**Directions:**

1. Preheat your oven to 200°C (400°F) and line a baking tray with parchment paper.
2. Dice the potatoes, zucchini, red bell peppers, eggplant, and carrots. Arrange them evenly on the baking tray.
3. Add the canned chickpeas and cherry tomatoes to the tray.
4. Place a whole garlic bulb on the tray.
5. Season the ingredients on the tray with salt and black pepper.
6. Place the tray in the oven and roast the vegetables and chickpeas for about 25-30 minutes, or until they are tender and slightly caramelized.
7. While the vegetables are roasting, prepare the sauce. In a blender or food processor, combine yogurt, crumbled feta cheese, paprika, cream cheese, and milk.
8. Add diced red chili peppers and parsley to the blender.
9. Once the garlic is roasted, squeeze the cloves out of their skins and add them to the blender.
10. Squeeze fresh lemon juice over the ingredients in the blender.
11. Add roasted or cooked onions to the blender.
12. Blend the ingredients until smooth to create a sauce.
13. Once the vegetables are roasted, remove the tray from the oven.
14. Spread the sauce evenly on a serving plate or in a bowl.
15. Spoon the roasted vegetables and chickpeas onto the sauce.
16. Garnish with fresh parsley and cilantro before serving.

**Notes:**

- The roasting time for the vegetables may vary depending on your oven and the size of the vegetable pieces. Check for tenderness and adjust the time as needed.
- The sauce can be adjusted to taste by adding more or less of the ingredients like lemon juice, paprika, or feta cheese.
- This dish can be served warm or at room temperature, making it versatile for different occasions.
- If you prefer a spicier dish, consider adding more red chili peppers or a pinch of cayenne pepper to the sauce.
